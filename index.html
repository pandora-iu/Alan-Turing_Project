About Him

Alan Mathison Turing was a English Mathematician and Computer Scientist. He was born on 23 June 1912 in London, England. He is called "The Father of Computer Science".

From a young age, Turing showed a particular interest in Mathematics and Science. At the age of 16, he was able to understand Albert Einstein's work and broadened the questioning of Newton's Laws of Motion from a Text in which this was never made explicit. By the age of 22, he is made a fellow at King's Collage for proving the central limit theorem in his dissertation.

Turing Test

A Turing Test is a test in artificial intelligence (AI) for determining whether or not a computer is capable of thinking like a human being. It was created by Alan Turing in 1950.

Tuing Machine

A Turing machine is a hypothetical machine created the mathematician Alan Turing in 1936. Despite its simplicity, the machine can simulate any computer algorithm.

Ferranti Mark 1 Programming System

Alan Turing designed the programming system of the Ferranti Mark 1, the world's first commercially available electronic digital computer!
